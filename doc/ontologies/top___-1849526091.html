  • rdfs:comment "This ontology adopts a 'deflationary' approach: the set of entities required to account for elements in the domain of discourse is minimal. For instance, a relationship (e.g. the connection of two persons) can also be an event, insofar the representation of its temporal properties is required. This way, there is no need of having an instance for the reified relation (continuant) and another one for its temporal aspects (occurrent). This implies that there's no disjointess at the level of basic categories such as 'continuants' and 'occurrents'. The resulting conceptualization trades metaphysics for natural language, by providing an easy accommodation for regular polysemy. At the cost of weakening the ontological import of the conceptualization, this is expected to improve linguistic usability."
