  • rdfs:comment "Concepts in this ontology are not intended to be 'categories', since they do not satisfy mutual exclusivity and general exhaustiveness. There's no disjointess at the level of basic concepts such as 'Continuant' and 'Occurrent', although disjointness may occur at lower levels.
    As a result, this ontology minimizes compatibility issues among different possible worlds. Also, this ontology is 'deflationary': there is no need of having different individuals to predicate different aspects of the same entity. For instance, there's no need to represent a book (object) and its content (information) as two separate individuals, even if nothing prevents to do that.
    Moreover, nothing prevents to supplement categorial axioms (disjointness), e.g. to sort out non intended models, as a separate layer."
